Contact Timing And Trajectory Optimization for Aggressive Aerial Acrobatics on Legged Robotics

EREE - Early Research Experience in E-Term @ WPI


In the Summer of 2023, I developed an optimal control framework (BiQuAcrobatics) for switched systems that enables quadrupeds and bipeds to walk and perform backflips. Optimal control is the process of finding a sequence of control inputs (actuator torques and forces) that minimize an objective (e.g., the energy used to execute the trajectory) and satisfy certain constraints (dynamics, friction constraints, actuator limits, etc.).


The backflipping problem is notoriously complex due to the harsh nonlinearities in the orientation dynamics of the floating base. To solve this problem, I parameterized the SO(3) rotation matrix with the history of the angular velocity and assumed a fixed contact sequence, including the contact timings as part of the decision variables. The work also uses pseudospectral (Legendre-Gauss-Radau) collocation for the transcription of the trajectory into a nonlinear problem solvable by Ipopt, which significantly improved solver convergence compared to naive shooting or forward Euler schemes.



To test the generality of my framework, I applied the methodology to two robots in simulation. It was tested on HURON (a bipedal robot developed by WPI), enabling the execution of a smooth human-like walking gait, and on Solo-12 (an open-source quadruped) for a series of acrobatic maneuvers, including backflips and spinning jumps. The next step is to test the framework on a real robot and publish the findings in a journal.
